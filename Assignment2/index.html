My Bio

Hello! My name is Michael Janczar. I am 21 years old and currently go to college at New England Institute of Technology. I also work part time at Igus Motion Plastics in East Providence and I'm a USA Hockey certified level 3 official. I have lived in Pawtucket, Rhode Isalnd, my entire life and went to Jacqueline M Walsh School for the Arts for high school. At JMW all students could pick from four "majors" from music, dance, theater, and visual art. I majored in visual and while I was there learned how to use Photoshop, After Effects, and a bit of Illustrator. I also learned more tradition analog art like acrylics, water color, and charcoal.

The first console I had was the Playstation 2 which I got for Christmas from my parents and that was the start of my wonderful addiction. I went from drawing on paper and building with legos to playing video games and challenging myself to 100% every title I had and then going back and trying to do it faster. At that time, some of my most favorite games were licensed platformers like SpongeBob SquarePants: Battle for Bikini Bottom and sports games like NHL 2k9. As I grew older, I moved away from console and now play on PC. Once I moved over to PC, around the age of 15-16, I didn't just have new games to play but a much more complicated system to learn, more freedom in what I could do on that system, and the ability to get into modding games. It was nothing crazy and I didn't know how to make the mods myself but the Minecraft modding/modpack community I think is what got me into game development. I would spend hours playing custom Minecraft modpacks which would add to the base game and give me more things to do. As I got older I would learn about the people who created these modpacks and follow their work and get into other games with healthy modding/mapping communities such as Call of Duty: Zombies and Black Ops 3: Mod Tools. I have never made a mod myself still to this day as the code aspect has always held me back but I love messing around in Roblox Studio and Blender making models and spending time teaching myself some model and level design through YouTube and tutorial sites.
